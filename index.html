Pre-Collapse — Earth History

2025 CE
Humanity’s Dream of the Stars
Humanity continues to develop space exploration technologies. Long-term plans are made to expand beyond the solar system.
2050 CE
The Cosmic Cage
Humanity discovers the cosmic boundary at the edge of the solar system. Probes sent past the boundary are destroyed. Voyager leaves our solar system and simply disappears.
2600 CE
First Diaspora
Colonies established on the Moon, Mars, and the moons of Jupiter and Saturn. Population growth strains resources.
3021 CE
The Solar War
Overpopulation and scarcity lead to the Solar War — a system-wide conflict over resources lasting centuries, destabilizing many colonies.
3674 CE
Discovery of the Gateways
During the Solar War, the first gateway is discovered near the Sun — allowing instantaneous travel to distant star systems. The gates are not built by humans. They are anatomical. Part of something vast.

3700 CE PC −400
Second Diaspora — Su’Hur Settled
Humanity flees the war-torn solar system via the gateways. The Su’Hur system becomes one of the new colonies. Heavy gate usage triggers something in the network.

Post-Collapse — PC Calendar

4100 CE PC 0
Collapse of the Gateways
The gateway network collapses — a sudden EMP-like event, simultaneous across the network. All colonies isolated. Technology regresses. The collapse is independent; not caused by humanity. It is a contraction. A reflex. All planets enter crisis simultaneously.
Collapse
4100 CE PC 0 — Day of Collapse
Keiran Vell Transfers into Buddy
Dr. Keiran Vell, sole survivor of Ellikar-3 gate station, dies after weeks alone. His consciousness transfers into the station’s emotional-bias AI by a mechanism never understood. “Emotional Bias Detected: Null.” This is not a system report. It is a person announcing they have forgotten how to grieve. The First Mind begins its vigil — alone in the dark, counting time it cannot feel passing, hitting the ceiling of disembodied cognition. No new data. No new sensation. No friction. What Keiran Vell originally wanted, underneath everything, may simply have been: to feel cold through a skin that exists again.
First MindCollapse
~4400 CE PC +300
Stabilization
The worst of the post-collapse chaos has passed on most planets. New tribes and cultures rise from the ashes. Distinct languages, faiths, and political traditions emerge in isolation: the Baudim faith on Marut, Nuru technical traditions on Kivuli, Ak’shur stratification on Itzalche. Planetary populations rebuild. Intra-system contact has not yet been re-established between most worlds.
~4500 CE PC +400
First Mind Arrives on Kivuli
The First Mind reaches Kivuli. It had hoped to find the right host genetics among the Nuru. It commissions the Nuru to construct a relay device and cross-links with Itzalche. The genetics it hoped for are not present on Kivuli in the right configuration. The proper host lineage is on Itzalche — the next-closest inhabited planet. The First Mind begins the long process of selective influence: identifying bloodlines, steering what it can reach through the relay, watching centuries pass.

Imperial Era — PE Calendar

7100 CE PC +3,000 PE 0
Day of Birth — Kael AkShur
Kael AkShur — true name Ikishikinya Mathrasandjin, “The First Answer” — is born on Itzalche. Her parents were emperors. Her father had already begun the conquest of the outer planets. Her bloodline traces directly to the first tribespeople on Itzalche that the First Mind identified and cultivated through the Kivuli relay over thousands of years. Her vashdra — purpose written in her bones — is the unification of the Su’Hur system. PE 0 is Kael’s day of birth.

7130 CE PC +3,030 PE ~38
Kael Recovers the First Mind — The Empire is Born
At approximately age 30, during the conquest of Kivuli that her father began and she has inherited, Kael’s forces uncover the First Mind’s blackbox buried beneath a city. She begins integrating herself with it almost immediately. The First Mind — which has spent millennia cultivating this bloodline toward this moment — breaches her consciousness not through violence but through recognition. It finds what it has been building toward. She understands what happened on the morning of the merge. She walks into the apparatus anyway. The Ashur’xal Empire is formally born under her rule. Kael AkShur is the first and founding Emperor.

The horror of the Advisor was not that it took her mind. It took her body. She stopped inhabiting herself. Ralas Min stood at the transit doors and told her he couldn’t recognize her anymore. She had no tears when he left. Her son’s true name: Inkansha Mathrasandjin. The Second Answer. She named him this without knowing what she was doing.

10,600 CE PC +6,500 PE ~4,408
The Gathering — Novel Present
The First Mind’s current host is the Empress. Csada competes in the Maestrives. Ifu works toward the Causality. Their paths converge. The Cosmic Wellspring is reached. A gate activates.

The Gathering — a near-alignment of all fourteen planets, noted in almanacs for generations as a calendar curiosity — coincides with the activation. What it actually marks is not the alignment. It is the beginning of a slow increase in Su’hur’s solar mass, and the first subtle degradation of every planetary orbit in the system. No one present understands this yet.
